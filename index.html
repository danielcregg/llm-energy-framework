Energy Consumption of Large Language Model Inference

A Multi-Architecture Benchmarking Study on GPU Hardware

Daniel Cregg · South East Technological University, Ireland

We present a rigorous framework for measuring LLM inference energy using direct NVML-based power sampling on an NVIDIA A100 80GB GPU. We benchmark 13 open-weight models (1B–70B parameters) across five architectural families—Llama, Mistral, Qwen, Phi, and Gemma—including pre-quantized GPTQ variants for large models. Our results reveal a sub-linear scaling law (E ∝ N0.91, R²=0.99), find that bitsandbytes quantisation now reduces per-token energy (reversing prior findings of 2.9–3.7× penalties), and demonstrate 2.9× variation in per-token carbon emissions depending on Irish grid conditions. All code, data, and reproducibility artifacts are publicly available.

Key Findings

Scaling Law

Energy scales as E ∝ N0.91 (R²=0.99) across architectures—higher than the single-family exponent of 0.80 found in prior Pythia-only studies, reflecting cross-architecture heterogeneity.

Batch Size Impact

Increasing batch size from 1 to 16 reduces J/tok by 9–15×, with larger models benefiting more from amortised weight-loading and kernel-launch overhead.

Quantisation Reversal

Bitsandbytes INT8/INT4 on Llama-3.1-8B now reduces energy by 10–20% at bs=1—reversing the 2.9–3.7× penalties found in prior work. Improved kernels eliminated the throughput bottleneck.

Carbon Variation

Using real Irish grid data (119–348 gCO2/kWh), per-token carbon emissions vary by 2.9× depending on time of day—exceeding the difference between some model choices.

Methodology

Layer 1: Hardware Instrumentation

NVML-based power sampling at 10 Hz in a daemon thread, with SLURM GPU index resolution and trapezoidal energy integration. 30-second idle baseline subtraction for net inference energy.

Layer 2: Inference Engine

Standardised model loading (FP16, INT8, INT4 via bitsandbytes; GPTQ-Int4 for large models) with torch.cuda.synchronize() for precise timing and greedy decoding.

Layer 3: Metric Computation

Computes J/tok, tok/s, mean/peak watts, and gCO2eq/tok from energy and inference measurements. 95% confidence intervals over 10 measured runs.

Layer 4: Orchestration

Benchmark protocol: 3 warmup + 10 measured runs per configuration with 5 diverse prompts (summarisation, QA, code, long-form, reasoning). Structured JSON output.

Hardware: NVIDIA A100 80GB PCIe (TDP 300W, idle ~59.8W) · Software: Python 3.11, PyTorch 2.10, Transformers, pynvml · Protocol: Greedy decoding, max_new_tokens=200, 10 runs × 5 prompts × 4 batch sizes
